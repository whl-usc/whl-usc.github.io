Crosslinking-based RNA Interactomes and Structuromes

The central resource hub dedicated to RNA-crosslinking methodologies and research.

Introducing the Crosslinking-based RNA Interactomes and Structurome (CRIS) database, a comprehensive resource advancing RNA research.

CRIS consolidates data from multiple pipelines, offering a user-friendly platform for researchers with varying computational expertise. It integrates data from established pipelines like Cross-linked RNA Secondary Structure Analysis using Network Techniques (CRSSANT), Selective 2'-Hydroxyl Acylation analyzed by Primer Extension (SHAPE), and Cross-linking and Immunoprecipitation (CLIP), ensuring consistent processing for accurate analysis. CRIS facilitates analyses across datasets, uncovering patterns in RNA structures, gene expression, RNA-RNA interactions, and providing insights into RNA dynamics and conformations.

Key features include detailed guidelines for data processing workflows, access to raw and processed data, statistical information, visualization tools, and quick access to relevant GitHub-linked scripts.
